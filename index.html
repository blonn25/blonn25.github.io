Hi, I'm Beau—a current Biophysics PhD student at UCSF! My current research interests are in computational approaches to biomolecular modeling, simulation, and design with broad applications across drug discovery, protein engineering, and synthetic biology. My research is also generously supported by an award through the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program.

I received my BS in Bioengineering with an Option in Data Science from the University of Washington in 2025. As an undergraduate researcher, I designed sequence-specific DNA-binding proteins and transcription factors as a member of the Baker Lab at the Institute for Protein Design. I also interned at Genentech as a Cell Therapy Process Engineer where I automated processes for stem cell therapy manufacturing and used molecular docking to identify lead compounds for future cancer drugs.

I grew up in the Pacific Northwest and like to stay active and get outdoors. I often spend my time training for triathlon by swimming, biking, and running, as well as hiking and paddle boarding. I also enjoy playing fantasy football and strategy board games in my free time.
